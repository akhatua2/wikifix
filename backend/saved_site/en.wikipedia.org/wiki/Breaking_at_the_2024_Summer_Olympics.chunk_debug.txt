<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Breakdancing" title="Breakdancing">Breakdancing</a> competitions at the <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2024_Summer_Olympics" title="2024 Summer Olympics">2024 Summer Olympics</a> ran from 9 to 10<span class="nowrap">&#160;</span>August at <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Place_de_la_Concorde" title="Place de la Concorde">Place de la Concorde</a>, marking the sport's official debut in the program and the first <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dancesport" title="Dancesport">dancesport</a> discipline to appear in Summer Olympic history.

--- Chunk 436 ---
<sup id="cite_ref-Paris_2024_1-0" class="reference"><a href="Breaking_at_the_2024_Summer_Olympics.html#cite_note-Paris_2024-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="Breaking_at_the_2024_Summer_Olympics.html#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Following its successful debut at the <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2018_Summer_Youth_Olympics" title="2018 Summer Youth Olympics">2018 Summer Youth Olympics</a> in <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Buenos_Aires" title="Buenos Aires">Buenos Aires</a>, breaking was confirmed as one of the three additional sports, along with <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sport_climbing_at_the_2024_Summer_Olympics" title="Sport climbing at the 2024 Summer Olympics">sport climbing</a> and <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Surfing_at_the_2024_Summer_Olympics" title="Surfing at the 2024 Summer Olympics">surfing</a>, approved for Paris 2024.

--- Chunk 437 ---
<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="Breaking_at_the_2024_Summer_Olympics.html#cite_note-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="Breaking_at_the_2024_Summer_Olympics.html#cite_note-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The competition saw a total of thirty-three breakers (sixteen b-boys and seventeen b-girls) staged in face-to-face single battles.

--- Chunk 438 ---
<sup id="cite_ref-breaking-qual_5-0" class="reference"><a href="Breaking_at_the_2024_Summer_Olympics.html#cite_note-breaking-qual-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> IOC president <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thomas_Bach" title="Thomas Bach">Thomas Bach</a> stated that breaking was added as part of an effort to draw more interest from young people in the Olympics.

<h2 id="Inclusion">

--- Chunk 442 ---
<span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Breaking_at_the_2024_Summer_Olympics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Inclusion"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span>

--- Chunk 443 ---
Breaking was introduced at the <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2024_Summer_Olympics" title="2024 Summer Olympics">2024 Summer Olympics</a> in Paris as an optional (temporary) sport.

--- Chunk 444 ---
Despite the United States being the birthplace of <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Breakdancing" title="Breakdancing">breakdancing</a>, the sport is not set to be included at the <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2028_Summer_Olympics" title="2028 Summer Olympics">2028 Summer Olympics</a> in Los Angeles; on its omission in the latter, the IOC's sports director Kit McConnell stated that "It's up to each local organizing committee to determine which [additional] sports to put forward that fit with their vision of the <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Summer_Olympics" class="mw-redirect" title="Summer Olympics">Games</a>.

--- Chunk 445 ---
Obviously, breaking fit very clearly with Paris's vision of a very youth-focused urban engagement."<sup id="cite_ref-Swaim_6-0" class="reference"><a href="Breaking_at_the_2024_Summer_Olympics.html#cite_note-Swaim-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> For LA 2028, <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Baseball" title="Baseball">baseball</a>, <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Softball" title="Softball">softball</a>, <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cricket" title="Cricket">cricket</a>, <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Flag_football" title="Flag football">flag football</a>, <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lacrosse" title="Lacrosse">lacrosse</a>, and <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Squash_(sport)" title="Squash (sport)">squash</a> were approved.

--- Chunk 446 ---
<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_DanceSport_Federation" title="World DanceSport Federation">World DanceSport Federation</a> (WDSF), the sport's governing body, was cited as working toward ensuring breaking's inclusion at the <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2032_Summer_Olympics" title="2032 Summer Olympics">2032 Summer Olympics</a> in Brisbane.

<h2 id="Competition_format">

--- Chunk 450 ---
Competition format

--- Chunk 451 ---
<span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Breaking_at_the_2024_Summer_Olympics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Competition format"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span>

--- Chunk 452 ---
The breaking competition comprised two gender-based medal events (one for men and the other for women) where sixteen B-Boys and sixteen B-Girls competed against each other.

--- Chunk 453 ---
The competition began with a <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Round-robin_tournament" title="Round-robin tournament">round robin</a> stage.

--- Chunk 454 ---
The 16 breakers were split in four groups and danced against the others in their group for a minute each.

--- Chunk 455 ---
The two best breakers from each group progressed to the <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Knockout_stage" class="mw-redirect" title="Knockout stage">knockout stage</a>, where breakers were eliminated from the competition after losing a one-on-one match.

Vocabulary: The number and variety of moves.

--- Chunk 464 ---
A high score requires a diverse set of moves in multiple positions.

--- Chunk 465 ---
Moves are grouped into <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Toprock" title="Toprock">toprock</a> (dance elements executed while standing), <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Downrock" class="mw-redirect" title="Downrock">downrock</a> (spinning on the floor, combined with footwork, drops and transitions) and freeze (a stop in an acrobatic position).

--- Chunk 466 ---
<sup id="cite_ref-:0_7-3" class="reference"><a href="Breaking_at_the_2024_Summer_Olympics.html#cite_note-:0-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>

--- Chunk 467 ---
Execution: The clean performance of moves, and the distinction of moves from one another so that they flow, but not blend, into another.

--- Chunk 468 ---
<sup id="cite_ref-:0_7-4" class="reference"><a href="Breaking_at_the_2024_Summer_Olympics.html#cite_note-:0-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>

--- Chunk 469 ---
Musicality: How well the breaker reacts to and expresses the music, which is provided by a tournament staff <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/DJ" class="mw-redirect" title="DJ">DJ</a> and not known to the breakers before the match.

--- Chunk 470 ---
<sup id="cite_ref-:0_7-5" class="reference"><a href="Breaking_at_the_2024_Summer_Olympics.html#cite_note-:0-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>

--- Chunk 471 ---
Originality: How the breaker "wows" the audience by making the dance their own.

--- Chunk 472 ---
<sup id="cite_ref-:0_7-6" class="reference"><a href="Breaking_at_the_2024_Summer_Olympics.html#cite_note-:0-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>

--- Chunk 473 ---
For each round, the judge casts a vote in favor of one of the competitors, with the majority vote getter winning that particular round.

--- Chunk 474 ---
Following the end of the match, the total number of rounds won and votes received are revealed.

--- Chunk 475 ---
<sup id="cite_ref-e739_8-2" class="reference"><a href="Breaking_at_the_2024_Summer_Olympics.html#cite_note-e739-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:0_7-7" class="reference"><a href="Breaking_at_the_2024_Summer_Olympics.html#cite_note-:0-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>

<h3 id="Placing">

--- Chunk 478 ---
<span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Breaking_at_the_2024_Summer_Olympics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Placing"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span>

--- Chunk 479 ---
In the round-robin stage, the competitors went head-to-head for two rounds.

--- Chunk 480 ---
They were eventually ranked by total number of rounds won, then number of total votes, and then seeding, with the top two in each group advancing.

--- Chunk 481 ---
The rest were pooled and sorted for 9th to 16th place with the same criteria.

--- Chunk 482 ---
<sup id="cite_ref-e739_8-3" class="reference"><a href="Breaking_at_the_2024_Summer_Olympics.html#cite_note-e739-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:0_7-8" class="reference"><a href="Breaking_at_the_2024_Summer_Olympics.html#cite_note-:0-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>

--- Chunk 483 ---
In the knockout stage, the competitors went head-to-head for three rounds.

--- Chunk 484 ---
Whoever won the most rounds won the match and advanced.

--- Chunk 485 ---
Those who lost in the quarterfinal were sorted by the number of knockout rounds won, then the number of knockout votes, and then the round-robin criteria.

--- Chunk 486 ---
Those who lost in the semi-finals competed in the bronze medal match.

--- Chunk 487 ---
<sup id="cite_ref-e739_8-4" class="reference"><a href="Breaking_at_the_2024_Summer_Olympics.html#cite_note-e739-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:0_7-9" class="reference"><a href="Breaking_at_the_2024_Summer_Olympics.html#cite_note-:0-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>

--- Chunk 491 ---
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1236090951">.mw-parser-output .hatnote{font-style:italic}.mw-parser-output div.hatnote{padding-left:1.6em;margin-bottom:0.5em}.mw-parser-output .hatnote i{font-style:normal}.mw-parser-output .hatnote+link+.hatnote{margin-top:-0.5em}@media print{body.ns-0 .mw-parser-output .hatnote{display:none!important}}</style>

--- Chunk 492 ---
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">

--- Chunk 493 ---
Main article: <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Breaking_at_the_2024_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Qualification" title="Breaking at the 2024 Summer Olympics – Qualification">Breaking at the 2024 Summer Olympics – Qualification</a>

--- Chunk 494 ---
A total of 32 quota places (sixteen each for B-Girls and B-Boys) were available for eligible dancers to compete for the inaugural medals in breaking.

--- Chunk 495 ---
NOCs could enter a maximum of four breakers (two per gender) across two medal events.

--- Chunk 496 ---
<sup id="cite_ref-breaking-qual_5-1" class="reference"><a href="Breaking_at_the_2024_Summer_Olympics.html#cite_note-breaking-qual-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="Breaking_at_the_2024_Summer_Olympics.html#cite_note-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>

--- Chunk 497 ---
Over eighty percent of the total quota was attributed to a large number of breakers through a tripartite qualification route.

--- Chunk 498 ---
First, the 2023 WDSF World Championships, scheduled for 23 to 24 September in <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Leuven" title="Leuven">Leuven</a>, <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Belgium" title="Belgium">Belgium</a>, awarded the B-Boy and B-Girl champion with a direct quota place for Paris 2024.

--- Chunk 499 ---
Second, a quintet of spots were assigned to the highest-ranked eligible breakers (one B-Boy and one B-Girl) competing in each of the designated continental meets (Africa, Americas, Asia, Europe, and Oceania), respecting the two-member NOC limit.

--- Chunk 500 ---
The remaining breakers were provided the final opportunity to book their slots for Paris 2024 through a four-month-long Olympic Qualifier Series, held between March and June 2024 in various locations worldwide.

--- Chunk 501 ---
<sup id="cite_ref-breaking-qual-1_11-0" class="reference"><a href="Breaking_at_the_2024_Summer_Olympics.html#cite_note-breaking-qual-1-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>

--- Chunk 502 ---
The host nation France reserved a spot each for a B-Boy and a B-Girl in their respective breaking events, while four more places (two per gender) were entitled to the eligible NOCs interested to have their breakers compete for Paris 2024 through a Universality invitation.

--- Chunk 503 ---
To be registered for a spot according to the criteria of the universality principle, breakers must have finished within the top 32 of their respective events in the final rankings of the four-month-long Olympic Qualifier Series.

<sup id="cite_ref-breaking-qual-1_11-1" class="reference"><a href="Breaking_at_the_2024_Summer_Olympics.html#cite_note-breaking-qual-1-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
